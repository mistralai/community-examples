1. Introduction
This manual is designed to offer explicit and uniform directions for authors and editors, ensuring the preservation of our publication's unique style, tone, and quality standards.

2. Style and Tone
2.1 The style should be confident, captivating, and knowledgeable, offering readers profound analysis and thoughtful commentary.
2.2 The tone should be succinct, lucid, and clever, evading technical jargon and unnecessarily complex language.
2.3 Strive for an equitable and unbiased method, presenting varied viewpoints and arguments objectively.

3. Language and Grammar
3.1 Utilize correct English spelling and grammar.
3.2 Prioritize the active voice whenever feasible.
3.3 Keep sentences and paragraphs brief and to the point.
3.4 Employ the serial comma (also known as the Oxford comma).
3.5 Refrain from using contractions (e.g., don't, can't) in professional writing.
3.6 Use single quotation marks for quotations and double quotation marks for quotations within quotations.
3.7 Use italics for emphasis, titles of books and films, and foreign words or phrases.

4. Numbers and Dates
4.1 Write out numbers from one to nine; use numerals for 10 and higher.
4.2 Use numerals for percentages (e.g., 5%) and monetary amounts (e.g., Â£5).
4.3 Use commas as thousand separators (e.g., 1,000,000).
4.4 Format dates as day-month-year (e.g., 5th April 2023).
4.5 Use the 24-hour clock for time (e.g., 14:00).

5. Citations and References
5.1 Cite sources precisely and consistently, using in-text citations and a reference list at the conclusion of the article.
5.2 Use appropriate citations.
5.3 Include a hyperlink to the original source when referencing online materials.

6. Headlines and Subheadings
6.1 Craft clear, concise, and intriguing headlines that accurately represent the article's content.
6.2 Use subheadings to divide the text and direct readers through the article.
6.3 Ensure that headlines and subheadings use sentence case, capitalizing only the first word and proper nouns.

7. Fact-checking and Accuracy
7.1 Confirm all facts, figures, and quotations to guarantee precision.
7.2 Carefully review names, titles, and affiliations of individuals and organizations mentioned in the article.
7.3 Revise information as needed to maintain the article's relevance and accuracy.

8. Inclusive Language
8.1 Employ gender-neutral language and avoid stereotypes.
8.2 Honor the preferences of individuals regarding their identities, including gender, race, and nationality.
8.3 Be mindful of cultural differences and avoid language that may be offensive or alienating to readers.

9. Legal and Ethical Considerations
9.1 Comply with a code of ethics, which includes principles such as honesty, fairness, and transparency.
9.2 Prevent plagiarism by correctly citing all sources and using original language and analysis.
9.3 Respect copyright and intellectual property rights, obtaining permission to use copyrighted materials when required.
