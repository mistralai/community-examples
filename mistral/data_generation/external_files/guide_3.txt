1. Introduction
This manual serves as a guide to provide clear-cut and consistent instructions for authors and editors, ensuring that our publication's distinct voice, tone, and editorial standards are upheld.

2. Style and Tone
2.1 The writing style should be confident, engaging, and well-informed, delivering readers with insightful analysis and commentary.
2.2 The tone should be brief, comprehensible, and clever, steering clear of industry jargon and convoluted language.
2.3 Maintain a fair and impartial stance, presenting diverse viewpoints and arguments evenhandedly.

3. Language and Grammar
3.1 Adhere to proper English spelling and grammar rules.
3.2 Opt for the active voice whenever possible.
3.3 Keep sentences and paragraphs brief and focused.
3.4 Include the serial comma (also known as the Oxford comma).
3.5 Avoid using contractions (e.g., don't, can't) in formal writing.
3.6 Use single quotation marks for quotes and double quotation marks for quotes within quotes.
3.7 Use italics for emphasis, book and movie titles, and foreign words or phrases.

4. Numbers and Dates
4.1 Write out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above.
4.2 Use numerals for percentages (e.g., 5%) and monetary values (e.g., Â£5).
4.3 Use commas as thousand separators (e.g., 1,000,000).
4.4 Present dates in the day-month-year format (e.g., 5th April 2023).
4.5 Use the 24-hour clock for time (e.g., 14:00).

5. Citations and References
5.1 Cite sources accurately and consistently, incorporating in-text citations and a reference list at the end of the article.
5.2 Use appropriate citation formats.
5.3 Provide a hyperlink to the original source when citing online content.

6. Headlines and Subheadings
6.1 Create clear, succinct, and captivating headlines that faithfully reflect the content of the article.
6.2 Employ subheadings to organize the text and help readers navigate through the article.
6.3 Ensure that headlines and subheadings use sentence case, capitalizing only the first word and proper nouns.

7. Fact-checking and Accuracy
7.1 Verify all facts, statistics, and quotes to ensure accuracy.
7.2 Double-check names, titles, and associations of individuals and organizations mentioned in the article.
7.3 Update information as necessary to preserve the article's relevance and accuracy.

8. Inclusive Language
8.1 Use gender-inclusive language and refrain from stereotypes.
8.2 Acknowledge the preferences of individuals regarding their identities, including gender, race, and nationality.
8.3 Be sensitive to cultural differences and avoid language that may be disrespectful or alienating to readers.

9. Legal and Ethical Considerations
9.1 Abide by a code of ethics, which encompasses principles such as honesty, fairness, and transparency.
9.2 Avoid plagiarism by properly crediting all sources and using original language and analysis.
9.3 Respect copyright and intellectual property rights, securing permission to use copyrighted materials when necessary.
