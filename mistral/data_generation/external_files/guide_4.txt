1. Introduction
This handbook aims to supply straightforward and uniform guidance for writers and editors, ensuring our publication's unique voice, style, and editorial standards are preserved.

2. Style and Tone
2.1 The writing should be self-assured, appealing, and well-versed, offering readers thought-provoking analysis and commentary.
2.2 The tone should be concise, understandable, and witty, avoiding specialized terminology and complex language.
2.3 Strive for balance and impartiality, presenting multiple perspectives and arguments fairly.

3. Language and Grammar
3.1 Follow standard English spelling and grammar conventions.
3.2 Use the active voice whenever appropriate.
3.3 Maintain sentences and paragraphs brief and coherent.
3.4 Implement the serial comma (also known as the Oxford comma).
3.5 Refrain from using contractions (e.g., don't, can't) in professional writing.
3.6 Use single quotation marks for quotations and double quotation marks for nested quotations.
3.7 Use italics for emphasis, titles of books and films, and foreign terms or phrases.

4. Numbers and Dates
4.1 Spell out numbers from one to nine; use numerals for 10 and higher.
4.2 Use numerals for percentages (e.g., 5%) and financial amounts (e.g., Â£5).
4.3 Use commas as thousand separators (e.g., 1,000,000).
4.4 Format dates as day-month-year (e.g., 5th April 2023).
4.5 Use the 24-hour clock for time (e.g., 14:00).

5. Citations and References
5.1 Cite sources precisely and consistently, featuring in-text citations and a reference list at the article's conclusion.
5.2 Adhere to proper citation guidelines.
5.3 Include a hyperlink to the original source when referencing online materials.

6. Headlines and Subheadings
6.1 Develop clear, brief, and engaging headlines that accurately represent the article's content.
6.2 Utilize subheadings to segment the text and assist readers in following the article.
6.3 Ensure that headlines and subheadings use sentence case, capitalizing only the first word and proper nouns.

7. Fact-checking and Accuracy
7.1 Confirm all facts, figures, and quotations to ensure precision.
7.2 Thoroughly review names, titles, and affiliations of individuals and organizations mentioned in the article.
7.3 Modify information as needed to maintain the article's relevance and accuracy.

8. Inclusive Language
8.1 Employ gender-neutral language and avoid stereotypes.
8.2 Respect the preferences of individuals regarding their identities, including gender, race, and nationality.
8.3 Be considerate of cultural differences and avoid language that may be offensive or alienating to readers.

9. Legal and Ethical Considerations
9.1 Uphold a code of ethics, which includes principles such as integrity, fairness, and transparency.
9.2 Prevent plagiarism by properly citing all sources and using original language and analysis.
9.3 Honor copyright and intellectual property rights, acquiring permission to use copyrighted materials when required.
